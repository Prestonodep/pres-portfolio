## Catalyst Explorer Research Notes

Catalyst Explorer is presented as a research and analytics platform for **Cardano’s Project Catalyst** ecosystem. Its purpose is to make previously fragmented public data easier to understand by turning dispersed spreadsheets, archives, proposal assessments, reports, and profile records into a more navigable product experience.[2][3]

The strongest publicly documented user problem is **information fragmentation**. The Lido Nation introduction explains that Catalyst participants previously had to hunt through multiple raw data sources to understand proposals, voting outcomes, reports, and community activity. Catalyst Explorer reframed that experience into searchable, filterable, structured exploration that helps users perform research, evaluate proposals, and follow project progress more effectively.[3]

Publicly described product capabilities include proposal exploration, charts, multi-layer filtering, profile claiming, following people and projects, update notifications, proposal assessment visibility, and better access to accountability data.[2][3] This creates a solid case-study narrative around designing for transparency, discovery, trust, and deep research in a complex governance ecosystem.

A visually observable product characteristic is that the live platform behaves like a **dense information system** with side navigation, theme controls, search, structured data categories, and dashboard-like exploration. That suggests Preston’s case study should likely emphasize product architecture, data usability, discoverability, and reducing cognitive overload for ecosystem participants.[2]

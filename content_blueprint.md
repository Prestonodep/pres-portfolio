# Preston Odep Portfolio Content Blueprint

## Design Direction

The site will fully commit to **Editorial Noir with Electric Lime Accents**. This means dark charcoal foundations, cool slate section breaks, oversized Playfair Display headlines, Plus Jakarta Sans for interface clarity, restrained acid-lime emphasis, editorial asymmetry, and evidence-led storytelling. The experience should feel like a hybrid of a premium digital magazine and a high-conviction product portfolio.

## Site Narrative

The site should present Preston Odep as a **multi-disciplinary designer with 8+ years of experience** who moves comfortably across product strategy, UI/UX, brand identity, website design, and event art direction. The conversion goal is not merely to show taste, but to prove strategic range, operational clarity, and business impact across African technology ecosystems.

## Navigation

Use the following primary navigation labels: **About, Work, Services, Skills, Contact**. The top-right action should read **Email Me** and anchor to the contact section on-page. The contact section itself will contain the mail action that launches the user’s email client.

### Project 1 — Solutech Limited

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Title | Solutech Limited — Designing a Sales Automation Platform Built for African Field Teams |
| Role | Product Designer |
| Category | Product Strategy · Enterprise UX · Sales Enablement |
| Summary | I worked as the product designer helping shape a sales automation experience used by teams operating across African markets. The challenge was translating the complexity of field sales, van sales, order workflows, merchandising, and reporting into a system that felt fast, usable, and decision-ready for distributed teams. |
| Problem | Sales and distribution operations often involve fragmented visibility, slow reporting loops, and workflow friction between teams in the field and decision-makers in-office. In environments where speed and clarity matter, poor product experience can slow down execution and reduce trust in the data itself. |
| Approach | I focused on simplifying operational complexity into clear task flows, modular dashboards, practical information hierarchy, and interfaces that support both action and oversight. The design emphasis was not just beauty, but confidence: helping users know what matters, what to do next, and where performance is trending. |
| Outcome framing | The resulting product direction supports easier field execution, better visibility, and stronger adoption of sales automation workflows in real operating environments. Solutech publicly positions the platform around AI-powered sales and distribution efficiency, enterprise adoption, and cross-country scale, making usability and trust central to product value.[1] |
| CTA label | View Case Study |

> This project sharpened my ability to design for operational environments where product decisions affect revenue, logistics, and field-team efficiency. My contribution sat at the intersection of usability, business logic, and trust — shaping a product that helps complex route-to-market activity feel clearer and more manageable.

### Project 5 — ShelfSense RT

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| Title | ShelfSense RT — A Real-Time Expiry Intelligence Concept for Modern Retail |
| Role | Product Strategist · UX Designer |
| Category | Concept Design · Retail Operations · Service Innovation |
| Summary | ShelfSense RT is a concept solution designed to help supermarkets reduce expiry losses by combining smart shelf monitoring, batch-level tracking, prioritised staff alerts, and actionable inventory workflows. |
| Problem | Expiring products often remain unnoticed until markdown windows are missed or waste has already occurred. Store teams may have inventory data, but not always the visibility, timing, or prioritisation needed at shelf level. |
| Approach | I designed the concept around a simple operational loop: detect products nearing expiry, prioritise action by urgency and value, guide staff on what to remove, discount, relocate, or replenish, and provide managers with live risk visibility across categories and branches. |
| Proposed solution | The product combines dashboard oversight, aisle-level task views, smart expiry warnings, and recommendation logic for markdowns, transfers, or stock rotation. The design borrows from proven retail operations patterns while packaging them into a more focused, user-friendly concept for supermarkets that need faster intervention on shelf risk. |
| Value proposition | The concept is designed to reduce waste, improve stock rotation, protect margin, and help store teams act earlier instead of reacting too late. |
| CTA label | View Case Study |

Suggested narrative paragraph:

> I included this concept to show how I think from first principles. When a business problem is operational, time-sensitive, and measurable, good design should make the right action easier to see and easier to execute.
